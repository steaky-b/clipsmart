The Review Process

Every clip is checked before you pay.

This is what separates performance UGC from just hiring random creators. You only spend budget on content that actually meets the brief.

01
Creator submits content
Creator posts the video on their own TikTok, Instagram, or YouTube account and submits the link through our platform. It's already live before review — that's by design.
02
We check the audience geography
We verify the creator's audience is at least 60% US (or your target region). If they don't meet the threshold, the clip is rejected — but stays live, generating free views you don't pay for.
03
We check the content quality
Is the content on-brand? Does it match the brief? Is it coherent, accurate, and non-misleading? Our team watches every clip. Off-brand or low-quality — rejected. No exceptions.
04
We wait for the view threshold
Once approved, we wait for the clip to hit the minimum view threshold (2,500 views by default). Only then does it start earning. This ensures only content with real engagement costs you anything.
05
Payout is calculated and capped
The creator earns their RPM rate on every qualified view — up to the per-clip payout cap. Even if one video gets 10 million views, your budget is protected.
06
You see it all in real time
Your campaign dashboard shows every approved clip, live view counts, budget used, and projected total reach — updated continuously for the duration of the campaign.
Approval Criteria

What gets approved.
What gets rejected.

Total transparency on how we decide. No grey areas — brands and creators both know the rules upfront.

✓ Approved — content gets paid
✓Matches the campaign brief in tone, product, and message
✓Creator's audience is 60%+ in the target region
✓Clip reaches or exceeds the minimum view threshold (2,500 default)
✓Content is accurate and not misleading about the product
✓Original content — not a repost or splice of another creator's work
✓Posted on the creator's own account, not a bought or ghost account
✗ Rejected — no payment, clip stays live
✗Audience below 60% in the target region (free views still accumulate)
✗Content doesn't reference the product or brand clearly
✗Off-brand: wrong tone, inappropriate context, misleading claims
✗Doesn't reach the minimum view threshold within the review window
✗Stolen or repurposed content from another creator or brand
✗Submitted after the campaign end date
